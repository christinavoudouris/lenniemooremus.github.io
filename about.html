Biography

For decades Lennie Moore has been a proven force as an accomplished composer, arranger and orchestrator of music for videogames, commercials, film, and television. His credits include Outcast, Outcast: Second Contact, Outcast 2: A New Beginning, Star Wars: The Old Republic, Kinect Disneyland Adventures, Halo Combat Evolved 10th Anniversary, Halo 2 Anniversary, Halo: The Master Chief Collection, Red Orchestra 2: Rising Storm, Rising Storm 2: Vietnam, artist music packs for DOTA2: The International 2016 and Counter Strike: Global Offensive, Fighter Within, The Walking Dead Motion Comic, Watchmen Motion Comic, MTG: Duels of the Planeswalkers, Dirty Harry, Dragonshard, and War of the Ring.

Lennie has also developed and taught Composition for Media as an adjunct professor for USC, UCLA Extension, and currently with the Technology and Applied Composition program at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music.

Lennie Moore began composing at an early age. “I remember being about 4 or 5 years old, sitting at the piano, with my mother transcribing on music paper for me, as I was plunking out a melody and saying things like ‘I want that note!’”

Lennie studied writing in his teens with jazz icons Toshiko Akiyoshi, Julian Priester, and Kim Richmond. At Berklee College of Music he completed his Bachelor of Music in Jazz Composition degree, studying with outstanding faculty Herb Pomeroy, David Mash, Ken Pullig, Barrie Nettles, Bob Freedman and Michael Gibbs. While living in Los Angeles, he studied orchestral conducting for 6 years under renowned teacher, William Kettering, who learned his craft from the conducting lineage of Leon Barzan and Arturo Toscanini.
